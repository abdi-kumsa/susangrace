# SUSANGRACE.COM — Website Design Brief for Figma Make

Design and build a fully responsive, 7-page personal brand and media-kit website for Susan Grace — a Kenyan media personality, podcast host, and women's empowerment movement leader. Her platform's manifesto is: **"Women of Purpose. Bold Faith. Unapologetic Self-Worth."** The audience is women aged 20–35 across Africa. The site must feel premium, cinematic, and emotionally bold — never like a templated SaaS or portfolio site. It will also be forwarded directly to brand partners and sponsors, so credibility matters as much as energy.

Where exact copy isn't provided, write original copy in Susan's voice: short, declarative, faith-and-action language, warm but bold — matching the tone of the attached document. Keep headlines punchy; keep body copy concise.

## BRAND IDENTITY SYSTEM

### Colors

| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Charcoal Black | `#15171B` | Dark section backgrounds, primary text on light backgrounds |
| Studio Grey-White | `#F2F0EE` | Default content background — warm off-white, not stark white |
| Pure White | `#FFFFFF` | Cards/content blocks needing max contrast pop |
| Blush Tint | `#FBEAEE` | Soft transitional section backgrounds |
| Live Red | `#FF2E45` | Tags, labels, small highlights ("FEATURED," category pills) |
| Raspberry Rose | `#C51C48` | Primary CTA/button color (pulled from Susan's own photography) |
| Indigo Night | `#0A0F2F` | One end of the "Vibrant Glow" gradient |
| Magenta Pulse | `#C02C6B` | Other end of the "Vibrant Glow" gradient |

**Vibrant Glow** = a soft radial/diagonal gradient blending Indigo Night → Magenta Pulse. This is used as a *lighting effect*, never as a flat full-section background fill: behind hero photography, at section-transition seams, behind the floating community wall, and on button/card hover glows.

### Typography

- **Headlines:** Archivo, Black/ExtraBold weight — bold geometric grotesque, large scale, confident.
- **Body text:** Inter, Regular/Medium weight — clean, highly readable.
- **Labels / eyebrows / tags:** Barlow Condensed, SemiBold, uppercase, letter-spaced — used for small category tags and section markers.

### Core Creative Concept: "Into the Light"

Every section behaves like something is being illuminated, deliberately, not like generic scroll-fade animations. This is the thread that ties all 7 pages together and is what makes the site feel custom-built. Five reusable motion patterns (defined once here, then referenced by name throughout this brief):

1. **Line-Build Reveal** — major headlines build line by line, each line "switching on" like a marquee bulb, rather than appearing all at once.
2. **Word-Morph** — paired transformation words (e.g. Fear → Faith) visually dissolve from the first word into the second as the user scrolls past, rather than being printed as static text with an arrow.
3. **Floating Fade** — images/cards appear at randomized positions within a defined safe zone, hold briefly, then fade out as new ones fade in elsewhere. Caps at 3–4 visible elements at once.
4. **Count-Up** — statistics animate upward (odometer-style) when scrolled into view.
5. **Glow Pulse** — the Vibrant Glow gradient softly brightens/dims behind key focal elements.

**Navigation:** Wordmark "SUSAN GRACE" in Archivo. Links: Home, About, The Women, Stories, Partner, Media Kit, Contact. ## PAGE 1 — HOME

**Hero:** Charcoal Black, full-bleed. Her photo sits large and off-center (not dead-centered). A Vibrant Glow pulses softly behind her, echoing real studio lighting from her own photography. Headline ("WOMEN OF PURPOSE. BOLD FAITH. UNAPOLOGETIC SELF-WORTH.") builds with Line-Build Reveal. Subheadline and supporting paragraph follow at smaller scale. Two CTAs: [Meet The Women] and [Partner With Susan]. The "170,394 women" stat appears via Count-Up, small and understated in a corner — confident, not shouty.

**What Susan Believes:** Background shifts to Studio Grey-White — a breath after the black hero. Each declarative line ("Faith builds. Faith creates. Faith leads. Faith takes action.") lands with increasing weight/size as the user scrolls, like a rising voice. The pull-quote ("What happened to your imagination?") sits oversized in front of a giant, faint quotation-mark watermark — sermon-poster energy, not a boring testimonial card.

**The Movement:** The five transformation pairs (Dreaming→Doing, Fear→Faith, Comparison→Purpose, Self-doubt→Self-worth, Waiting→Action) each use the Word-Morph pattern as the user scrolls past — the first word visibly dissolves into the second. This is the single most distinctive interaction on the site. Stats block below uses Count-Up on Charcoal Black or Blush Tint cards.

**The Ripple Effect (Community Voices):** A large, still "THE WOMEN" headline anchors the center of the section. Real community screenshots use the Floating Fade pattern around it — drifting in, holding, fading out, capped at 3–4 visible at once — with a soft Glow Pulse behind the whole wall. Closing statement + [Read More Stories] CTA leads into the full Women page.

**The Stories (teaser):** Headline "Women Building The Future," supporting body copy, two CTAs [Nominate A Woman] / [Become A Guest], leading into the full Stories page.

**Work With Susan (teaser):** Headline "Bring Susan Into The Conversation," partnership list, CTA [Partner With Susan], leading into the full Partner page.

**About Susan (teaser):** Headline "Meet Susan Grace," short bio excerpt, CTA [Read Full Story], leading into the full About page.

**Final Section:** Full-bleed Charcoal Black again — the site bookends itself here. Manifesto line returns once more like a closing stamp. Simple email capture (First Name, Email Address, button "Join The Community"). No clutter.

## PAGE 2 — ABOUT SUSAN

**Header:** Full-bleed portrait (photo #2) on Charcoal Black, name and role overlay, subtle Glow Pulse.

**Full biography:** Long-form editorial text block on Studio Grey-White. First letter of the opening paragraph styled as a large Archivo drop-cap in Raspberry Rose — a magazine-profile touch. Generous line height for long-form readability.

**Media journey:** A vertical timeline component where each career milestone is a small light point connected by a thin line — nodes "switch on" via Line-Build Reveal as the user scrolls, reinforcing the "Into the Light" concept as literally her path being lit up moment by moment.

**Philosophy & Mission:** Paired manifesto-style blocks on Blush Tint, short declarative lines similar to the Home "What Susan Believes" treatment.

**Personal story:** An intimate, quieter section. Charcoal Black background with a single warm Glow Pulse spot — literally a spotlight on her personal story. First-person voice. Smaller, warmer photo (#5).

**Closing CTA:** Leads into Partner or Contact.

## PAGE 3 — THE WOMEN

This is the full expansion of "The Ripple Effect." The floating screenshot wall becomes page-wide rather than a single section, with a low-opacity version of it running as a subtle background texture throughout the whole page so it feels continuously alive.

**Community stories & testimonials:** Filterable by theme using Barlow Condensed pill tags (Business, Faith, Confidence, Career). Filtering is a tactile, immediate interaction.

**Success stories:** Presented as expandable cards — collapsed state shows a photo and one-line outcome in Raspberry Rose; expanding reveals the full story. Keeps a content-heavy page scannable.

**Video submissions:** A horizontal-scroll carousel of vertical 9:16 video thumbnail cards (matching the short-form format her own audience is native to), with a glowing play button on hover.

## RESPONSIVE BEHAVIOR

On mobile: hero text stacks above the photo rather than beside it. Floating screenshot wall caps at 2 visible elements instead of 3–4. Horizontal filmstrips (Stories page) become vertical swipe carousels. All tap targets minimum 44px. Navigation becomes the full-screen overlay described above.

---

## WHAT TO AVOID

No generic 3-icon feature grids. No purple-to-blue SaaS gradient hero clichés. No dead-centered hero text over a stock photo. No uniform card grids where every card has identical rounded corners and the same drop shadow. No identical fade-in-on-scroll applied uniformly to every element with zero variation. No default rainbow-colored social media icons. No generic checkmark-circle stat icons. The site should feel editorial and cinematic — bespoke, not templated.
